<p><p>Residential flooring underlayment types include Cement Backer Units (CBU), polyethylene, rubber, cork, plywood, and others. CBU is ideal for moisture-prone areas, providing a stable base for tile. Polyethylene offers moisture resistance and soundproofing benefits. Rubber underlayment excels in shock absorption and insulation. Cork is eco-friendly, offering thermal insulation and sound dampening. Plywood serves as a sturdy base but offers limited moisture resistance. Other materials, such as foam and fiberboard, cater to diverse flooring needs, enhancing comfort and performance.</p></p>
