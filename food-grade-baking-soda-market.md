<p><p>The food-grade baking soda market includes several production methods: </p><p>1. **Sodium Carbonate Method** involves the reaction of sodium carbonate with water and carbon dioxide, producing sodium bicarbonate suitable for food applications. </p><p>2. **Trona Method** utilizes a naturally occurring mineral to extract sodium bicarbonate through a purification process, enhancing its food safety profile. </p><p>3. **Ammonium Carbonate Method** involves reacting sodium chloride with ammonium bicarbonate, resulting in a high-purity baking soda, ideal for the food industry. Each method impacts quality and cost dynamics in the market.</p></p>
