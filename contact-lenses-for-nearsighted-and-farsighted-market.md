<p><p>The market for contact lenses is divided into two main types based on vision correction needs: nearsightedness (myopia) and farsightedness (hyperopia). Nearsighted contact lenses are designed for individuals who struggle to see distant objects clearly, focusing light in front of the retina. Conversely, farsighted contact lenses cater to those with difficulty seeing nearby objects, allowing light to focus directly on the retina. Both segments encompass various lens types, including daily disposables, monthly wear, and specialty lenses, to address specific vision requirements.</p></p>
