<p><p>Conductive stainless fibers are classified into two main market types: long fibers and short fibers. Long fibers, typically over 10 millimeters, offer enhanced flexibility and are commonly used in textiles, providing excellent conductivity for applications like smart clothing and wearable technology. Short fibers, generally under 10 millimeters, are primarily utilized in composites and coatings, enhancing electrical properties while maintaining structural integrity. Both types cater to diverse industries, including automotive, electronics, and aerospace, driven by the growing demand for advanced, conductive materials.</p></p>
