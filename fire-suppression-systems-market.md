<p><p>Fire suppression systems are categorized into four main types: chemical, gaseous, water, and foam. Chemical suppression systems use agents that interrupt combustion, ideal for sensitive environments. Gaseous systems deploy inert gases to displace oxygen, effective in electronics and data centers. Water-based systems use sprinklers or hoses, suitable for general fires, while foam systems create a barrier over flammable liquids, preventing vapor release. Each type serves specific applications, addressing diverse fire risks across various industries.</p></p>
