<p><p>Metal forming in the automotive market encompasses various techniques used to shape metal into desired forms for vehicle components. Roll forming involves continuous bending of metal sheets into shapes. Stretch forming stretches metal over a mold, creating complex curves. Stamping uses dies to cut or shape metal sheets. Deep drawing involves pulling metal into a deeper cavity for parts like cups. Hydroforming uses high-pressure fluid to mold metal, allowing for intricate shapes. Other methods may include extrusion and forging, enhancing design flexibility.</p></p>
