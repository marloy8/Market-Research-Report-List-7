<p><p>Personal air sampling pumps are categorized into two primary types based on their flow rates: high flow and low flow. High flow pumps, typically operating at rates above 1 liter per minute, are designed for sampling larger volumes of air and are suited for the detection of contaminants in occupational settings. In contrast, low flow pumps operate at rates below 1 liter per minute, offering more precise sampling for specific applications, such as detecting volatile organic compounds in ambient air. Each type serves distinct monitoring needs.</p></p>
